Top 10 Things You Need to Know About the Do Not Call Registry

October 15, 2023 Admin 24 Comments

The National Do Not Call Registry is a valuable tool for reducing unwanted telemarketing calls, but many people don't fully understand how it works. Here are the top 10 things you should know about the DNC registry:

  1. It's free to register - There is no charge to add your number to the registry.
  2. Registration is permanent - Your number stays on the registry until you remove it or discontinue service.
  3. It takes 31 days for registration to take effect - Telemarketers have 31 days to update their calling lists.
  4. Some calls are still permitted - Political calls, charities, surveys, and informational calls are still allowed.
  5. Businesses must comply - Most telemarketers are required to follow DNC regulations.
  6. You can file complaints - If you receive illegal calls, you can report them to the FTC.
  7. Cell phones can be registered - Both landlines and mobile numbers can be added to the registry.
  8. It applies nationwide - The registry is federal and applies across the United States.
  9. There are exceptions - Companies you have an existing relationship with may still call for a limited time.
  10. It reduces but doesn't eliminate calls - While helpful, the registry won't stop all unwanted calls.

Understanding these key points will help you make the most of the Do Not Call Registry and manage your expectations about what it can and cannot do.

How the DNC Registry Affects Business Calling Practices

October 10, 2023 Admin 18 Comments

For businesses that rely on telephone marketing, compliance with the Do Not Call Registry is not just a best practice—it's the law. The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) and the FTC's Telemarketing Sales Rule establish strict requirements for telemarketers.

Here's how the DNC registry affects business calling practices:

1. Scrubbing Calling Lists

Businesses must regularly scrub their calling lists against the National Do Not Call Registry. This process must be done at least every 31 days to ensure compliance.

2. Record-Keeping Requirements

Companies must maintain records of their compliance with DNC regulations for at least five years, including documentation of their scrubbing processes.

3. Established Business Relationship Exception

Businesses may call customers with whom they have an established business relationship for up to 18 months after the last purchase or payment, or 3 months after an inquiry or application.

4. Written Consent Exception

Companies may call consumers who have provided written consent to receive calls, even if their number is on the DNC registry.

Failure to comply with DNC regulations can result in significant penalties—up to $43,792 per violation. That's why it's essential for businesses to understand and follow these rules carefully.

5 Benefits of the Do Not Call Registry You Might Not Know About

October 5, 2023 Admin 32 Comments

While most people know that the Do Not Call Registry helps reduce telemarketing calls, there are several additional benefits that aren't as widely recognized:

1. Reduced Risk of Scams

By reducing the number of telemarketing calls you receive, the registry also reduces your exposure to potential phone scams. Many scams begin with unsolicited calls.

2. Peace and Quiet

Fewer interruptions during family time, meals, and relaxation periods contribute to better quality of life and reduced stress.

3. Protection for Vulnerable Populations

The registry offers added protection for elderly individuals who may be more susceptible to fraudulent schemes or high-pressure sales tactics.

4. Environmental Impact

By reducing unwanted calls, the registry indirectly reduces energy consumption associated with phone use and time spent on unwanted conversations.

5. Increased Productivity

For professionals who work from home or rely on their phones for business, fewer interruptions mean increased productivity and focus.

These additional benefits make the Do Not Call Registry an even more valuable resource than many people realize.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for my number to be registered on the Do Not Call list?

After you register your phone number, it takes 31 days for your number to appear on the Do Not Call Registry. Telemarketers have up to 31 days to update their calling lists once your number is added to the registry.

What types of calls are not blocked by the registry?

The Do Not Call Registry does not block calls from:

  • Political organizations
  • Charities
  • Telephone surveyors
  • Companies with which you have an established business relationship
  • Companies you've given written permission to call you
  • Informational calls (such as appointment reminders)
Is there a fee to register my number on the Do Not Call Registry?

No, it is completely free to register your number on the National Do Not Call Registry. The Federal Trade Commission operates the registry, and there is no charge for consumers to add their numbers. Be wary of any service that tries to charge you for registration.

How do I report violations of the Do Not Call Registry?

If you receive a telemarketing call from a company that you believe is violating the Do Not Call Registry rules, you can file a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission:

  1. Visit www.donotcall.gov
  2. Click on "Report a Violation"
  3. Provide the date of the call and the phone number or name of the company that called you

The FTC reviews complaints and takes action against companies that repeatedly violate the rules.

Does my registration on the Do Not Call Registry expire?

No, your registration on the National Do Not Call Registry does not expire. Once you register your phone number, it will remain on the registry until you remove it or discontinue service. There is no need to renew your registration.

Can I register my cell phone on the Do Not Call Registry?

Yes, you can register both landline and wireless phone numbers on the National Do Not Call Registry. The registry accepts cell phones and has since it was opened to wireless numbers in 2004.

Will the Do Not Call Registry stop all unwanted calls?

While the Do Not Call Registry can significantly reduce the number of telemarketing calls you receive, it won't stop all unwanted calls. The registry only applies to legitimate telemarketers. It does not stop calls from:

  • Scammers and illegal robocallers
  • Political organizations
  • Charities
  • Survey companies
  • Companies with which you have an existing business relationship

For scam calls, you may need additional call-blocking solutions.

How can businesses access the Do Not Call Registry?

Businesses that engage in telemarketing must subscribe to the National Do Not Call Registry and download the list of registered numbers. There is a fee for accessing the registry, which varies based on the area codes accessed. Businesses must scrub their calling lists against the registry at least once every 31 days.
